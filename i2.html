The Tomsk Musketeer Regiment was located on the territory of today's Lagerny Sad ("Camp Garden") in the 18th century, and in the 19th century there were the summer camps of the military regiment, hence the name of the Lagerny Sad itself. Usually, during the training time, ordinary citizens could not get here, but by the end of the 19th century, dances, performances and charity evenings began to be arranged in camps in the "garden". All of it was often on an open stage. Lagerny Sad was accessible to the public with the permission of the military authorities, with tickets given out by officers. Music and dance performances were arranged here in the summer military meeting room once a week. And sometimes, various communities were organizing paid festivities for charitable purposes.
